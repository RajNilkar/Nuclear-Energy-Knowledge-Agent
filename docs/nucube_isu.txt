NuCube Energy and Idaho State University Selected for U.S. Department of Energy Nuclear Energy Launch Pad USA (April 29, 2026)

NuCube Energy, Inc. announced that the Advanced Research and Test (ART) Reactor project, jointly submitted by Idaho State University (ISU) and NuCube, has been selected by the U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) to participate in the National Reactor Innovation Center's Nuclear Energy Launch Pad USA, one of the nation's most sought-after programs for accelerating advanced nuclear reactor technologies from concept to deployment.

About Nuclear Energy Launch Pad

The U.S. Department of Energy's Office of Nuclear Energy and the National Reactor Innovation Center (NRIC) announced the first developers selected for the newly established Nuclear Energy Launch Pad. The initiative helps private nuclear developers move their technologies from concept to commercial deployment.

This selection represents a significant milestone for both NuCube and Idaho State University, positioning the ART Reactor project as one of the first advanced nuclear technologies to be accelerated through this flagship federal initiative.

NuCube and ISU Collaboration

The joint submission between NuCube Energy and Idaho State University leverages NuCube's innovative microreactor technology with ISU's research capabilities and infrastructure. This partnership demonstrates the close relationship between the company and the university, both located in Idaho, and their shared commitment to advancing nuclear technology.

NuCube's Arizona Expansion (May 4, 2026)

Following the Nuclear Energy Launch Pad selection, NuCube also announced the establishment of its initial on-the-ground presence in Phoenix, Arizona, and the appointment of Michael Green as Chief Legal Officer. This expansion builds on the momentum from the DOE selections and the company's growing portfolio of partnerships and government program participation.
